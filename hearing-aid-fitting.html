WHO THIS IS FOR

Signs a hearing aid might help

Hearing loss is usually gradual, which is why most people notice the effects before they notice the loss itself. These are the patterns we hear most often from patients before they come in for an assessment.

In conversation

Asking people to repeat themselves, especially in groups or with background noise. Following conversation feels effortful rather than automatic. Volume on the TV or phone calls set higher than others find comfortable.

Day to day

Missing the doorbell, phone, or someone calling from another room. A sense of withdrawing from social situations because following along is tiring. Family members mentioning your hearing more than once.

THE PROCESS

From hearing test to everyday hearing

Fitting is not a single appointment — it's a short process of measurement, adjustment, and review until the device matches how you actually hear and live.

01 — ASSESSMENT

Hearing test

Pure Tone Audiometry establishes your hearing thresholds across frequencies — the baseline every later decision is built on.

02 — SELECTION

Device trial

Based on your test results, lifestyle and budget, you trial one or more hearing aid types in real environments, not just the clinic.

03 — FITTING

Fitting & programming

The device is programmed to your specific thresholds and physically fitted, with real-ear measurement used to confirm the output matches the prescription.

04 — REVIEW

Fine-tuning

Follow-up visits adjust settings based on your real-world feedback — most patients need one or two tuning sessions in the first month.

WHAT CHANGES

What a properly fitted hearing aid actually changes

A device that's fitted and programmed correctly — not just switched on — tends to produce results patients describe consistently.

  • ✓ Conversations in groups and noisy rooms become easier to follow, not just louder
  • ✓ Less listening fatigue at the end of the day
  • ✓ TV, calls and music return to a comfortable, shared volume
  • ✓ Reduced need to ask people to repeat themselves
  • ✓ For many patients, a noticeable reduction in associated tinnitus awareness
